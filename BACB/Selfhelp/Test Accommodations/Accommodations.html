Accommodations

The BACB (Behavior Analyst Certification Board) provides accommodations to ensure that you have equal access to the exam. Accommodations are approved on a case-by-case basis for individuals with documented disabilities or health conditions, in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

To request accommodations, you must submit the required documentation and receive approval before scheduling your exam.

Accommodation types

BACB offers a range of accommodations depending on your needs. Some of the common accommodations include:

  • Extended testing time
  • Additional breaks
  • Access to medical devices
  • Screen magnification or contrast adjustments
  • Paper-based exams (for eligible individuals)
  • Separate testing room
  • Use of assistive technology (e.g., screen readers)

If you need accommodations not listed above, contact the BACB to discuss your specific needs. All requests will be considered as long as they are supported by appropriate documentation.

Request accommodations

To request accommodations, you must follow these steps:

  1. Submit a certification application to the BACB.
  2. Once you receive an Authorization to Test (ATT) email, submit a formal request for accommodations using the documentation provided on the BACB Accommodations Documentation page.
  3. Provide all necessary supporting documentation, such as medical or psychological evaluations, with your request.
  4. Wait for approval before scheduling your exam.

Make sure to request accommodations as early as possible after receiving your ATT email. Late requests may delay your ability to schedule the exam.

Where can I find more information about accommodations?

For more information on the accommodations process, required documentation, and types of accommodations offered, visit the BACB Accommodations Documentation page.

If you have further questions or need assistance with the accommodations process, you can contact the BACB directly via their Contact Us page.
