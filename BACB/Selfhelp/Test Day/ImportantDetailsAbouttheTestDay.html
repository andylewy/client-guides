Before the test

It is important to arrive at the test center at least 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment time to allow enough time for check-in procedures. Pearson VUE wants you to have the best testing experience possible. On exam day, give yourself enough time to get to the test center.

Be sure to review your Authorization to Test (ATT) email for specific instructions about your test day.

What do I need to bring?

On the day of the test, you must bring the following:

  • Two forms of identification (ID) — one primary and one secondary:
    • The primary ID must be a government-issued ID with a photograph and signature (e.g., driver’s license, passport).
    • The secondary ID must also include a signature (e.g., Social Security card, bank card, employee ID).

Make sure that both IDs are valid and not expired.

Required identification

Primary ID:

Your primary ID must meet these criteria:

  • Government-issued
  • Unexpired
  • Contain both a photograph and a signature

Accepted forms of primary ID include:

  • Driver's license
  • Passport
  • Military ID
  • Alien registration card (green card)
  • State or country ID card

Secondary ID:

Your secondary ID must also be unexpired and include a signature. Examples of secondary ID include:

  • Social Security card
  • Bank card
  • Employee ID
  • Library card

Make sure that both your primary and secondary IDs match the name on your registration.

Test center directions

You can find detailed directions to your test center location in the confirmation email from Pearson VUE or by logging into your Pearson VUE account. Make sure to plan your route ahead of time and account for possible traffic or public transportation delays.

Day-of-test emergencies

If you encounter an emergency that prevents you from attending the test, contact the BACB through their Administrative Appeals page immediately. The BACB may allow you to reschedule the test depending on the nature of the emergency. Emergencies reported less than 48 hours before the exam may not qualify for rescheduling, so it is important to notify BACB as soon as possible.
